# **4\. Caption & Script Framework**

## **4.1 Instagram Caption Structure**

Every caption should follow this flow:

1. Hook (first line) — must stop the scroll. Bold claim, question, or shocking stat. This is the most important line.

2. Story/value body (3–6 short paragraphs) — the meat. Either a personal story, a lesson, or actionable tips. Keep paragraphs to 1–2 sentences max.

3. Soft CTA — natural mention of eSIM Launch or link in bio. Never feels like an ad. Example: “the exact platform I used is linked in my bio if you wanna check it out”

4. Engagement closer — question or prompt to drive comments. Example: “what’s stopping you from starting? drop it below ⬇️”

## **4.2 TikTok Script Structure**

Keep it 15–60 seconds. The structure:

**First 3 seconds:** Hook that creates curiosity or tension. “I made $40K last month selling something you’ve never heard of.”

**Middle (10–40 sec):** Quick explanation, story beat, or tip. Fast-paced, no filler words.

**End (5–10 sec):** CTA — either “follow for more” or “link in bio” or “comment X and I’ll send you the guide.”

# **8\. Automation Pipeline**

## **8.1 Overview**

The goal is a semi-automated pipeline that generates and distributes content with minimal manual intervention, transitioning to full automation once quality is validated.

## **8.2 Pipeline Steps**

### **Step 1: Content Generation (LLM)**

Use the Anthropic API (Claude) with Carmelo’s personality baked into the system prompt. Generate batches of: Instagram captions (10–15 per week), TikTok scripts (7–10 per week), blog posts (2–3 per week), email newsletters (1 per week). Store all generated content in a local folder structure organized by platform and date.

### **Step 2: Image Generation (LoRA)**

Use the trained LoRA model to generate images that match each piece of content. Pair captions with appropriate image settings. Manual review at first to ensure quality and consistency.

### **Step 3: Content Review (Semi-Auto Phase)**

During the semi-automated phase, all generated content (text \+ images) goes into a review queue. Review for: voice consistency, factual accuracy, image quality, CTA placement. Approve, edit, or reject. Only approved content moves to scheduling.

### **Step 4: Scheduling & Posting**

Use automation tools (Make.com, n8n, or Buffer) to schedule approved content across Instagram and TikTok. Recommended posting times for Miami/US audience: Instagram 11am–1pm and 7–9pm EST, TikTok 10am–12pm and 6–8pm EST.

### **Step 5: Email Automation**

Set up through Resend, ConvertKit, or Mailchimp. Welcome sequence triggers on Lesson 1 signup. Weekly newsletter sends every Tuesday at 10am EST. All emails written in Carmelo’s voice using the same LLM system prompt.

### **Step 6: Blog Publishing**

Auto-publish AI-generated blog posts to carmeloreyes.com. Each post is SEO-optimized with target keywords and contains 3–5 natural backlinks to esimlaunch.com. Schedule 2–3 posts per week.
